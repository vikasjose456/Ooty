Must-see in Ooty

Ooty - Resort town in the
Western Ghats mountains

Toy Train

The Nilgiri Mountain Railway is a narrow-gauge train from Mettupalayam to Ooty in southern India. As soon as the toy train pulls out of the station, passengers are transfixed by the spectacular mountain scenery surrounding them. The train track winds through rolling green hills, plunges into remote valleys, and passes by verdant farmland and forests, offering views that are breathtaking in their beauty.

Doddabetta Peak

It is the third highest peak in South India at 2,637 metres (8,652 ft). There is a reserved forest area around the peak. It is a popular tourist attraction with road access to the summit. It is the third highest peak in South India next to Anamudi and Meesapulimala. The word Doddabetta is derived from Kannada, meaning 'big hill'.

Shooting point

The Shooting Spot offers a panoramic view of the Nilgiris as well as the vast expanse of undulating meadows covered with shola and pine forests. The serene ambience of Shooting Spot, enriched with the scent of pine forests and eucalyptus, makes it a perfect spot for enjoying a picnic or a day out with friends and family.

Pykara Waterfalls and Boating

The Pykara is a river 19 kilometres from Ooty. It rises at Mukurthi peak, flows northwards, and then turns to the west after reaching the edge of the plateau. Lamb's Rock Coonoor

It is a point on the way to Dolphin's Nose, and the Seat was so called by the then Collector Mr. E.B. Thomas, after one Captain Lamb who made earnest attempts to open a path to the place. It is situated in Burliar village and is about 8 kilometre from Coonoor. It was discovered by a British army officer Lamb, hence the name Lamb's Rock

Dolphin Nose Coonoor

The place gets its name because of the protruding flat rock that resembles the nose of a dolphin. It is from this point that you can get the best view of the valleys beneath. A visit to this view point is surely promising as the view itself is not the only offering it gives.
